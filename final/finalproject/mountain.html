Rinjani Mountain

Gunung Rinjani, located on the island of Lombok in West Nusa Tenggara, is one of Indonesiaâ€™s most iconic volcanoes and the second-highest mountain in the country, standing at 3,726 meters above sea level. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, Rinjani features lush forests, steep rocky trails, and the famous Segara Anak Crater Lake, which lies within its massive caldera. Many hikers visit Rinjani to experience challenging multi-day trekking routes that offer panoramic views, sunrise vistas, and the opportunity to witness geothermal hot springs near the crater lake. Despite its beauty, Rinjani is an active volcano, and the surrounding area is protected as part of Gunung Rinjani National Park. Visitors are encouraged to hike responsibly, follow local regulations, and respect the natural environment to help preserve this extraordinary mountain for future generations.
